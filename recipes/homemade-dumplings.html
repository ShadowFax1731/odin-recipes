Homemade Dumplings

A dumpling is a small mass of dough that can be boiled, fried, baked, or steamed. There are a ton of dumpling varieties out there, from pork- and cabbage-filled Chinese dumplings to cheesy Polish dumplings.

These boiled drop dumplings, which are popular in the U.S., are often paired with chicken and broth to create a popular comfort food called chicken and dumplings.

They're prepared by dropping spoonfuls of dough into a pot of boiling soup or stew. The starch in the dumplings thickens the broth, resulting in a rich stew that's impossible to resist.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Baking Powder
  • White Sugar
  • Salt
  • Butter or Margarine
  • Milk

Steps

  1. These dumplings start with all-purpose flour, which creates structure and holds the other ingredients together.
  2. Baking powder is a leavening agent, which means it releases gas that makes the dough expand. It's responsible for the dumplings' light and fluffy texture.

  3. A teaspoon of sugar adds an extremely subtle hint of sweetness. It also helps the dumplings retain their moisture through the cooking process and slows gluten development, which ensures tender dumplings.

  4. A pinch of salt seasons the dumplings and enhances the flavors of the other ingredients.
  5. Cold butter or margarine is cut into the flour mixture to bind the dry ingredients together. It also keeps the dumplings light and tender.
  6. Whole milk helps create a smooth, moist dough.
